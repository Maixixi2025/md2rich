Search Console Social Tracking 2026: For Markdown Creators

On July 7, 2026, Google added a new “Social & Video Platforms” filter group to the Search Performance report inside Search Console. The filter breaks your existing search impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position down by the platform that surfaced the link - LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, Reddit, Quora, and a handful of forum hosts. For a creator who drafts in Markdown and cross-posts to social platforms, this is the missing measurement layer for a workflow you have been running on intuition for two years.

Before this filter, a LinkedIn post that ranked in Google and drove traffic back to your site was indistinguishable from a Reddit mention or a Hacker News submission inside Search Console - all of them landed in the “Web” filter under generic search appearance. The July 7 launch is the first time Google exposes the platform as a first-class dimension, which means you can finally answer the question: which social platform is sending me the search traffic, and which queries are triggering it.

What changed on July 7, 2026

Three things changed inside the Performance report for any verified property:

  1. A new “Social & Video Platforms” filter group appeared next to the existing Search type, Date, Query, Page, Country, and Device filters.
  2. Impressions on a URL are now attributed to the platform filter when the user first encountered the URL inside a LinkedIn post card, an X status embed, a YouTube description link, a TikTok bio link, or a Reddit thread that Google surfaced as a “Discussions and forums” unit.
  3. The click that brings the user back to your property still counts against your URL-prefix or domain property as before - the social filter is additive, not a replacement.

In practice, the filter shows up as a small chip under the chart titled “Social & Video Platforms” with toggles for LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, Reddit, Quora, Stack Exchange, and a handful of forum hosts. Selecting one or more chips filters the entire report to the impressions and clicks that originated from that platform.

Why this matters for Markdown-first creators

A Markdown-first publishing workflow looks like this in 2026:

  1. Draft the article in a plain text editor - Obsidian, iA Writer, VS Code, or a plain .md file.
  2. Convert the Markdown to rich text via md2rich - this step runs entirely in the browser, no upload, no account, no third-party server.
  3. Paste the rich text into LinkedIn, an X Article, a Medium draft, a Substack post, or a Bluesky long-form post - whichever surface the audience reads on.
  4. Include a link back to the canonical version on your own site.

Until July 7, step 4 produced zero telemetry in Search Console. The link in the LinkedIn post drove traffic back to the canonical URL, and that traffic showed up as generic “Web” search or as a Direct entry - both unfiltered by where the user first saw the link. The new filter fixes this: when a user finds your LinkedIn post in Google Search, the impression is now attributed to LinkedIn, and when the user clicks through to your canonical article, the click registers as having arrived via LinkedIn-driven search appearance.

How to read the new filter today

Open Search Console, pick your property, and go to Performance > Search Results. The chart looks the same as before. Scroll down to the filter row and look for a new chip group labeled “Social & Video Platforms”.

Three readings to take first:

  • Sort by impressions, group by social platform - the table at the bottom of the report shows clicks and impressions; toggle the social filter to LinkedIn to see which queries are driving LinkedIn-originated impressions, then toggle to X to compare. If LinkedIn drives 8x more impressions than X for the same query set, the LinkedIn cross-post is the discovery surface worth investing more time in.
  • Compare CTR between platforms - if LinkedIn cards drive 12 impressions and 1 click (CTR 8.3%) but X Articles drive 14 impressions and 0 clicks (CTR 0%), the X title card is not matching search intent and needs a rewrite.
  • Find orphaned platforms - if the filter shows zero impressions for TikTok even though you have a bio link there, either the post was not indexed, or the rich-text format you published was not the version Google surfaced. Try re-publishing with a different hook.

Limitations to know about

The new filter is a useful first step, but it has three limitations as of July 8, 2026:

  • Limited platform list. LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, Reddit, Quora, Stack Exchange, and a handful of forum hosts. Mastodon, Bluesky, Threads, and Substack are not yet in the filter - even though they appear in Google's “Discussions and forums” unit, their impressions are still attributed to the generic Web filter.
  • One impression per URL per session. If a single user sees your LinkedIn card and then your X Article card for the same query, Google counts two impressions but they may appear in different filter groups. There is no deduplication across platform filters.
  • No public API for the social filter yet. The Search Console API supports the standard dimensions (query, page, country, device, search appearance, date). The new social_platform dimension is in the UI first; API support is expected in a follow-up quarter.

The first limitation is the one that matters most for a Markdown-first creator: if you publish to Bluesky long-form, your impressions will still show up under “Web” for now. Google has hinted that the platform list will expand later in 2026.

Quick start: three steps to use the filter today

  1. Open Search Console for your verified property. Go to Performance > Search Results. Wait for the “Social & Video Platforms” filter to appear (rolling out between July 7 and July 21).
  2. Toggle LinkedIn first, since LinkedIn cards tend to drive the most search impressions for text-heavy cross-posts. Sort the table by impressions, scan the top 10 queries, and check whether each query's CTR is above 2%.
  3. Cross-check the URLs - take the URLs that appear in the LinkedIn filter, open each one in your blog's analytics, and confirm the link is still live and still renders correctly. The conversion you ran with md2rich is the format Google indexed; if the rich-text paste ever broke (LinkedIn changed its accepted subset, for example), the impressions will dry up within a week.

The bigger picture

Search Console has historically been a tool for site owners. The July 7 launch is the first time Google has formally recognized that the search results page is now a mix of organic web URLs and platform-native embeds, and that the platform-native embeds deserve their own measurement layer. For a creator who publishes across multiple platforms, this is the validation that the multi-platform workflow is a first-class publishing strategy, not a workaround for not having a big enough audience on any single platform.

The Markdown-first publishing workflow - draft locally, convert locally with md2rich, paste into whatever platform the audience reads - is exactly the workflow this filter was built to measure. If you have been running it without telemetry, today is the first day you can see what is working.

FAQ

What did Google Search Console launch on July 7, 2026?

Google added a new “Social & Video Platforms” filter group inside the Search Performance report. You can now break down search impressions, clicks, CTR, and position by LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, and a small set of other platforms that surface Google's search results as embedded units.

Does this track how my LinkedIn post ranks in Google search?

Yes. When a LinkedIn post that contains a URL you control appears as a “people also viewed” card or a discussion-and-forums unit in Google Search, that impression is now attributed to the LinkedIn platform filter in Search Console, and the click that the user makes back to your URL is recorded in your property.

Do I need a verified TikTok or YouTube channel in Search Console to see this data?

No. The new social filter works against the web property you have already verified. Google matches impressions on LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, Reddit, Quora, and a handful of forum hosts to your existing property; you do not need to claim a separate social handle inside Search Console.

What should a Markdown creator check first inside the new panel?

Start with the “social_platform” dimension on the Performance report, sort by impressions, and look for queries where your article URL appears inside a LinkedIn or X embed. Those impressions are the search-discovery signal for the platform-cross-post workflow you have already built; if the CTR is below 2 percent, the article's title or thumbnail is not matching the searcher intent.

How does md2rich fit into this tracking picture?

md2rich is the conversion step between your Markdown draft and the rich-text payload that LinkedIn, X Articles, Medium, and Substack accept. When that payload includes a link back to your own property, the new social filter is what makes the search-discovery loop visible: you can see the query that triggered the LinkedIn card, the URL the user clicked, and the page that received the click, all without leaving Search Console.

Is the social panel available for every Search Console property?

Yes for verified domain and URL-prefix properties. Google is rolling the filter out between July 7 and July 21, 2026. If you do not see it on the Performance page yet, give it a few days or refresh the property status.
